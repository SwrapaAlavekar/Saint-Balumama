Saint Balumama

A revered shepherd-saint remembered for simple living, devotion, kindness, and service to ordinary people.

Explore His Story

His Life

A saint from a humble shepherd family

Saint Balumama, born Ballappa in 1892 at Akkol near Chikkodi, is deeply respected across Maharashtra and Karnataka. He came from a shepherd community and spent much of his life close to village people, nature, and devotional practice.

Devotees remember him as a guide who cared for the poor, lived with simplicity, and inspired people to keep faith while doing good work in daily life.

Quick Facts

  • Birth name: Ballappa
  • Born: 3 October 1892
  • Birthplace: Akkol, Karnataka
  • Samadhi: 4 September 1966
  • Main shrine: Adamapur, Kolhapur district

Adamapur Mandir

The place of devotion

The main temple dedicated to Saint Balumama stands at Adamapur in Maharashtra's Kolhapur district. It is visited by devotees who come for darshan, prayer, and remembrance of his teachings.

The shrine is especially connected with Balumama's samadhi and his sacred flock of sheep, a living part of the tradition cherished by many followers.

Legacy

Remembered across villages and generations

Saint Balumama's story continues through bhajans, books, temple visits, local traditions, and the devotion of families who pass his message from one generation to the next.
